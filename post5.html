How to Prepare for Your First ABRSM Exam

Published on July 28, 2025

Preparing for your first ABRSM (Associated Board of the Royal Schools of Music) exam can feel both exciting and daunting. With the right approach, you can turn your hard work into a successful and rewarding experience. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you prepare effectively.

1. Understand the Syllabus

The first step is to thoroughly understand the syllabus for your specific grade and instrument. The ABRSM website provides detailed information on the required pieces, scales, sight-reading, and aural tests. Make sure you and your teacher have the most current syllabus.

2. Choose Your Pieces Wisely

You'll need to perform three pieces from different lists (A, B, and C). Work with your teacher to select pieces that you enjoy and that showcase your strengths. Liking your pieces will make practice much more enjoyable.

3. Master Your Scales and Arpeggios

Scales are a fundamental part of the exam and demonstrate your technical control. Practice them daily until they are fluent, even, and musically shaped. Don't just play the notes; think about the tone and dynamics.

4. Practice Sight-Reading Regularly

Sight-reading can be one of the most challenging parts of the exam. The key is consistent practice. Spend 5-10 minutes each day playing a new, simple piece of music. Look for the key signature, time signature, and any patterns before you start playing.

5. Develop Your Aural Skills

Aural tests assess your listening skills. Use ABRSM's official apps and practice materials to test yourself on clapping rhythms, identifying changes in pitch, and singing back melodies. This is something you can practice even away from your instrument.

6. Perform Mock Exams

Simulating the exam environment is one of the best ways to prepare for the pressure of the day. Ask your teacher to conduct mock exams. This will help you manage your nerves and get used to performing all the components of the exam in one sitting.

By following these steps and maintaining a consistent practice schedule, you'll be well on your way to success in your first ABRSM exam. Good luck!
